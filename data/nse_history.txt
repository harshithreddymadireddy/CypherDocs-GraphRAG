# National Stock Exchange of India (NSE): Inception, Technological Disruptions, Scandals, and Growth Timeline (1992 - 2026)

## 1. Overview and Inception (1992 - 1994)
The National Stock Exchange of India Limited (NSE) is the country's leading stock exchange and the world's largest derivatives exchange by trading volume. Its establishment reformed the Indian capital markets, introducing transparency, accessibility, and modern financial infrastructure.

* **Inception Background:** In the early 1990s, the Indian stock market was dominated by the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE), which operated as a closed association of brokers using a manual, open outcry floor trading system. The system lacked transparency, suffered from settlement delays, and was vulnerable to manipulation, culminating in the Securities Scam of 1992 (the Harshad Mehta Scam).
* **Pherwani Committee Recommendations:** In response, the Government of India appointed a High Powered Committee on the Establishment of New Stock Exchanges, chaired by M.J. Pherwani. The committee recommended the creation of a national-level stock exchange owned by financial institutions to act as a model exchange and promote competition.
* **Incorporation:** NSE was incorporated in November 1992 as a tax-paying corporate entity, separating ownership (institutions) from trading rights (brokers). This demutualized structure was revolutionary for India.
* **Commencement of Operations:** 
  - **Wholesale Debt Market (WDM) segment:** Launched in June 1994.
  - **Capital Market (equities) segment:** Launched in November 1994.
  - **Derivatives segment:** Launched in June 2000.
* **Founding Institutions:** Promoted by leading Indian financial institutions, including the Industrial Development Bank of India (IDBI), Industrial Credit and Investment Corporation of India (ICICI), Industrial Finance Corporation of India (IFCI), State Bank of India (SBI), Life Insurance Corporation (LIC), and General Insurance Corporation (GIC).

## 2. Technological Leadership and Market Infrastructure
NSE's primary contribution to Indian finance was the complete technological overhaul of trading infrastructure.

### NEAT (National Exchange for Automated Trading)
* **The Technology:** NSE introduced the NEAT system, a fully automated, screen-based, order-driven trading system. It replaced the physical trading floor with an electronic limit order book, where buy and sell orders are automatically matched based on price-time priority.
* **Impact:** NEAT brought absolute transparency. For the first time, investors across the country could see the exact best bid and ask prices and order depth in real time, eliminating broker-dictated pricing spreads.

### Satellite (VSAT) Communication Network
* **The Infrastructure:** To expand trading nationwide, NSE deployed a network of Very Small Aperture Terminal (VSAT) satellite links. 
* **Impact:** This broke Mumbai's monopoly on financial trading. Brokers in smaller cities (Tier-2 and Tier-3) could install a VSAT dish and connect directly to the central NSE trading engine, integrating the entire country into a single, unified national market.

### Dematerialization and NSDL
* **The Problem:** In the mid-1990s, stock trading relied on physical paper share certificates. Settlements took weeks, and markets were plagued by bad delivery, theft, forgery, and lost certificates.
* **The Solution:** In 1996, the NSE, alongside IDBI and UTI, promoted the National Securities Depository Limited (NSDL), India's first depository. 
* **Impact:** It enabled the dematerialization (demat) of shares, converting paper certificates into electronic ledger balances. This eliminated physical share handling, reduced the settlement cycle from T+15 days down to T+2, and eventually to T+1 by 2023.

## 3. Key Scandals, Reforms, and Operational Incidents
Despite its success, NSE has faced significant operational challenges and regulatory crises.

### The 2012 Emkay "Fat Finger" Flash Crash
* **Date:** October 5, 2012.
* **The Incident:** An institutional dealer at Emkay Global Financial Services made a "fat finger" keyboard entry error while executing a basket order for a client. Instead of placing an order worth millions, the system executed 59 erroneous trades valued at approximately ₹650 crore ($120 million) on the Nifty index, causing it to plunge by 15.5% (nearly 900 points) within seconds.
* **The Outcome:** The market hit a circuit breaker and halted. NSE refused to annul the trades, forcing Emkay to bear the financial loss. This incident prompted SEBI to mandate stricter risk-management controls, pre-trade limit checks, and dynamic price bands on individual order sizes.

### The Co-location (Co-Lo) Algorithmic Trading Scam
* **Timeline:** 2015 - 2022 (initiated by whistleblower letters in 2015).
* **The Mechanism:** In 2010, NSE introduced a co-location facility where high-frequency trading (HFT) brokers could place their servers inside the exchange premises for low-latency market access. NSE distributed market feed data using a sequential Tick-by-Tick (TBT) protocol.
* **The Manipulation:** Whistleblowers alleged that certain brokers (specifically OPG Securities) colluded with NSE insiders to connect first to the backup data servers. Because the server processed feeds sequentially, the broker connecting first received price ticks fractions of a millisecond before the rest of the market, generating massive arbitrage profits.
* **Regulatory Fallout:** SEBI launched an extensive investigation. It was discovered that former MD & CEO Chitra Ramkrishna had shared confidential exchange data and consulted a mysterious "Himalayan Yogi" on key management decisions, including the appointment of Anand Subramanian as Group Operating Officer.
* **Reforms:** The scam led to the resignation and subsequent arrest of Chitra Ramkrishna and former MD Ravi Narain by federal agencies. NSE was fined hundreds of crores, banned from accessing securities markets for a period, and forced to transition to a Multicast Tick-by-Tick (MTBT) system to distribute market feeds simultaneously to all participants.

## 4. Chronological Evolution and Milestones

- **1992 (November):** NSE is incorporated as a corporate financial entity.
- **1993 (April):** NSE is recognized as a stock exchange under the Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956.
- **1994 (June):** Commenced operations in the Wholesale Debt Market (WDM) segment.
- **1994 (November):** Commenced equity trading on the Capital Market (CM) segment.
- **1995 (October):** NSE becomes the largest stock exchange in India by trading volume, surpassing the 120-year-old Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE).
- **1996 (April):** Launched the NIFTY 50 index (initially called NSE-50).
- **1996 (November):** Established National Securities Depository Limited (NSDL) to pioneer demat trading.
- **1998 (May):** Launched the Nifty Junior Index (now Nifty Next 50).
- **2000 (April):** Launched Internet-based trading in India, allowing retail investors to place trades via web browsers.
- **2000 (June):** Commenced derivatives trading with the launch of Index Futures.
- **2001 (June):** Launched Index Options trading.
- **2001 (November):** Launched Single Stock Futures.
- **2008 (August):** Commenced Currency Derivatives trading.
- **2010 (January):** Introduced the Co-location server facility for high-frequency algorithmic traders.
- **2011 (January):** Launched mutual fund trading platform (NSE NMF II).
- **2012 (October):** The Emkay "Fat Finger" trade triggers a 15% flash crash, leading to SEBI's pre-trade check mandates.
- **2015 (January):** Whistleblower exposes the Co-location server manipulation scam.
- **2016 (December):** CEO Chitra Ramkrishna resigns amid intensifying co-location investigations.
- **2019 (April):** SEBI issues major orders against NSE in the co-location probe, ordering disgorgement of profits.
- **2021 (February):** Technical glitch shuts down NSE for nearly 4 hours due to telecom link failures at primary data centers.
- **2022 (July):** Ashishkumar Chauhan is appointed MD & CEO of NSE. Formerly a founding member of NSE, he returned after a successful stint as CEO of BSE.
- **2023 (January):** Indian equity markets transition to T+1 settlement cycle, led by NSE and BSE, making India the first major economy to settle equity trades within 24 hours.
- **2024 - 2026:** NSE consolidates its position as the world's largest derivatives exchange. Option volumes hit record highs, prompting regulatory interventions (SEBI guidelines on index derivatives) to curb speculative retail derivative exposure. Active investor accounts registered with NSE surpass 9 crore (90 million).

## 5. Governance and Ownership Structure
* **Demutualized Model:** NSE operates under a demutualized corporate model. Unlike traditional broker-owned exchanges, NSE's brokers are strictly clients and cannot own shares in the exchange or sit on the board.
* **Major Shareholders:** NSE is owned by a consortium of domestic banks, insurance corporations, financial institutions, and foreign investment funds. Major investors include Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC), State Bank of India (SBI), Stock Holding Corporation of India, and global private equity funds.
* **Current Leadership:** Led by Chairman Girish Chandra Chaturvedi and MD & CEO Ashishkumar Chauhan, who has focused on stabilizing technical infrastructure, resolving regulatory disputes, and overseeing the long-awaited NSE Initial Public Offering (IPO).
